Max's Story: The Seed of Inspiration

In the heart of a children's home in Beitou, there was a young child who had experienced life's hardships from the very start. His mother was sentenced to seven years in prison shortly after his birth, leaving him to navigate the world with only the memory of her love. But despite his longing, his mother never reached out, and this absence created a void that seemed insurmountable.

Yet, within the walls of the children's home, Max found moments of happiness. Building colorful Lego masterpieces became his refugeâ€”a place where love and connection knew no boundaries. While other children went home to their families during holidays, he remained, creating a world of imagination and creativity with the tiny bricks.

Max shared the children's home with his two brothers and one sister. Although they offered occasional visits, their past experiences of living with their mother softened the blow of her absence. For Max, however, the yearning for a mother's love and understanding only grew stronger.



Problem Statement



Inadequate Care and Support for Children of Incarcerated Parents in Taiwan

In Taiwan, current legislation and practices for children of incarcerated mothers pose significant challenges to their well-being and development. According to statistics from the Ministry of Justice's Correctional Agency in November 106 (2017), there are 40 children under the age of 3 residing in correctional institutions with their incarcerated mothers.

These children face several critical issues during their stay in prison. At night, they are confined together with their mothers and other adult inmates in shared cells, lacking dedicated facilities for their specific needs. Moreover, during the daytime, the children must accompany their mothers to work in the prison's factories, forcing the mothers to juggle the responsibilities of parenting and labor duties.

The shortage of correctional officers in Taiwan's correctional facilities further exacerbates the situation. With a staff-to-inmate ratio of 1:11, correctional officers struggle to provide adequate supervision and care for both the adult inmates and the young children in their custody.

Furthermore, the guidelines for allowing incarcerated individuals to return home for visits create limited opportunities for children to maintain meaningful connections with their incarcerated mothers. The infrequency of visitation, determined by the length of the sentence, imposes emotional strain on both mothers and children, hindering their ability to bond and offer each other the necessary support.



The Need for Change: Enhancing the Lives of Children of Incarcerated Mothers

The current system fails to provide an environment conducive to the healthy development and emotional well-being of children living in correctional institutions with their mothers. The absence of dedicated facilities and appropriate care arrangements for these young souls leads to potential risks to their physical safety and emotional stability.

To create a brighter future for these children and empower them to break the cycle of incarceration, it is imperative to address the following key issues:

1. Child-Friendly Facilities: The establishment of dedicated spaces within correctional institutions, designed to cater to the specific needs of children, is crucial. These facilities should ensure a safe, nurturing environment that encourages learning, creativity, and healthy social interactions.

2. Comprehensive Support: A comprehensive support system should be in place to assist incarcerated mothers in balancing their roles as parents and inmates. This includes access to parenting education, mental health counseling, and assistance with childcare responsibilities during work hours.

3. Increased Staffing: Addressing the severe shortage of correctional officers is vital to ensure the safety and well-being of both adult inmates and their children. Adequate staffing ratios will enable officers to provide personalized care and attention to those in their charge.

4. More Frequent Visitation: Facilitating more frequent and extended visitations between children and their incarcerated mothers is essential for maintaining strong family bonds and supporting the children's emotional development.

Goals and Values

Our Mission: Building Bridges, Changing Lives

At the Mockingbird Initiative, we strive to connect children with their incarcerated parents in meaningful and transformative ways. We recognize that education is a powerful tool that can open doors to brighter futures. Through our tutoring programs, we support the children's academic journey, nurturing their thirst for knowledge and personal growth.

But our mission goes beyond the classroom. We organize charity bake sales to fund the tuition of these young souls, ensuring they have access to quality education and a path towards a better tomorrow.

The Power of Connection: Letters Beyond Walls

One unique aspect of our initiative lies in the exchange of heartfelt letters and photos between the children and their parents in prison. We know that love knows no barriers, and through these letters, children can express their feelings and thoughts creatively. A simple "XO" becomes a symbol of love and warmth, bridging the gap between hearts separated by prison walls.
